| Concept                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             | Example                                                                                                                                                                   |
| ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Noun-Adjective Agereement**<br>ADJECTIVES MUST ALWAYS AGREE WITH THE NOUNS THEY MODIFY IN *GENDER, NUMBER*, AND *CASE*.                                                                                                                                           | **nauta malus** (both noun and adj. are masc. sing. nom.)                                                                                                                 |
| **Substantive Use of the Adjective**<br>1. ADJECTIVES USED AS NOUNS ARE CALLED *SUBSTANTIVES*.<br>2. TRANSLATION OF A SUBSTANTIVE MUST INDICATE GENDER AND NUMBER.                                                                                                  | ***Bonus multa* videt.**<br>*The good man* see many things.                                                                                                               |
| **Predicate Adjective**<br>1. APPEARS WITH A COPULATIVE VERB<br>2. ALWAYS AGREES WITH THE NOUN IT MODIFIES IN *GENDER, NUMBER*, AND *CASE*                                                                                                                          | ***Clārus* est poēta.**<br>The poet is *famous*.                                                                                                                          |
| **Subject Infinitive**<br>AN INFINITIVE (A NEUTER SINGULAR VERBAL NOUN) MAY BE THE SUBJECT OF ANOTHER VERB.                                                                                                                                                         | **Bonum est *labōrāre*.**<br>*To work* is good.                                                                                                                           |
| **Ablative of Personal Agent**<br>1. EXPRESS THE PERSON OR AGENT BY WHOM AN ACTION IS PERFORMED<br>2. OCCURS WITH VERBS IN THE *PASSIVE* VOICE<br>3. REQUIRES **Ā/AB**                                                                                              | **Fīlius *ā regīnā* vocābātur.**<br>The son was being summoned *by the queen*.                                                                                            |
| **Ablative of Manner**<br>1. EXPRESS THE WAY OR MANNER IN WHICH AN ACTION IS PERFORMED<br>2. REQUIRES **CUM** WHEN NOT MODIFIED BY AN ADJECTIVE<br>3. MAY USE (BUT DOES NOT REQUIRE) CUM WHEN MODIFIED BY AN ADJECTIVE<br>4. MAY BE TRANSLATED WITH AN "-LY" ADVERB | ***Cum dīligentiā* labōrat.**<br>She works *with diligence / diligently.*<br>***Magnā (cum) dīligentiā* labōrat.**<br>She works *with great diligence / very diligently*. |
| **Appositive**<br>1. DEFINES OR LIMITS ANOTHER NOUN NEXT TO WHICH IT IS PLACED<br>2. ALWAYS AGREES WITH THE NOUN IT DEFINES IN *CASE*                                                                                                                               | **Horātius, *poēta* magnus, multōs librōs habet.**<br>Horace, a great *poet*, has many books.                                                                             |

[^1]: Some adjectives have *no* ending for the masculine singular nominative (e.g. **miser**, **pulcher**). The masculine singular nominative must be memorized as part of the vocabulary entry.
